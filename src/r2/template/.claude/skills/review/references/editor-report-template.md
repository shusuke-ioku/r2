## Calibration Guidance

### On NVI Ratings
The NVI framework captures the three things editors actually weigh. A paper scoring 5/5/2 (highly novel, highly valid, low importance) may struggle more than a 3/5/5 (modestly novel, highly valid, highly important). "Where is the theory?" criticisms from reviewers often mask importance concerns — the real question is "why should I care?", not "where is your formal model?"

### On the Publication Assessment
Be brutally honest. Calibrate against what actually gets published. A paper with two fatal issues and five serious ones does not get "R&R likely at a top journal." A paper with zero fatal issues, two serious issues with moderate-feasibility fixes, and a clear contribution might. Most submitted papers are rejected — that is the baseline, not the exception.

### On the Revision Roadmap
Rank by impact on publication prospects, not by severity alone. A serious issue with an easy fix should rank higher than a serious issue requiring fundamental redesign, because the author can actually address it.

### On Contradictions
If R1 says the framing is the paper's strength and R3 says the framing misrepresents the case, that is a real tension. Do not paper over disagreements — adjudicate them.

### On Strengths
Reviewers who only criticize are not helpful. The strengths section tells the author what to keep, what to build on, and what is already working. A paper that does three things well and two things poorly needs to know which is which.
